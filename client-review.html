06Recommendations to Improve Lead Quality

Five specific changes that target the gap between leads delivered and leads quoted. Each one is designed to put better prospects in front of your team.

Our focus for the next 30 days

Quote rate is the highest-leverage metric we can move right now. Raising it from 6.7% to 12–15% would double the volume of quotes at the same lead cost — and the binds follow. Here's how we get there.

1

Tighten Auto targeting to higher-intent prospects

Auto is delivering volume but the quality mix is uneven. We'll layer in tighter filters on the segments that historically don't quote — prior carrier type, coverage level, and household vehicle count — so that leads reaching your team are pre-qualified as shoppers, not price-checkers. Your existing exclusions on SR-22, DUI, and poor credit stay in place; this is an additive tightening on the top 20% of uneconomic segments.
→ Expected impact: Auto quote rate lifts from 5.5% toward 10%. Fewer, better leads.
2

Concentrate spend on our proven winners

Four sources are doing the heavy lifting: Home Tier 3, Home Tier 2, Home Search, and Auto Email. Together they account for the majority of your contacts and all of your sales. We'll shift budget away from low-return sources (where the market is simply more expensive than we can profitably compete) and consolidate it on the winners. This is how we turn a broad campaign into a focused one.
→ Expected impact: Higher average lead quality, better CPA on both LOBs.
3

Prioritize bundle (Auto + Home) prospects

Your 175% bid modifier on Multi-Vehicle + Home prospects is the right move — bundled households close at higher rates and carry more premium. We recommend layering a small upward adjustment on the "already bundled" signal too, so we're leaning into the prospects most likely to make a full switch. This is a lead-mix change, not a bid increase in aggregate.
→ Expected impact: Higher bind rate, bigger average premium per policy.
4

Speed-to-contact coaching

At 64% contact rate you're doing well — industry average is 20–40%. The lift from here comes from speed: leads contacted within 5 minutes quote at roughly 2x the rate of leads contacted after an hour. We can share lead-delivery timing data with your team so you can measure response time and optimize the first-touch window.
→ Expected impact: Higher contact-to-quote conversion without any extra spend.
5

Investigate why contacted leads aren't quoting

Of the 123 prospects your team reached, only 13 received a quote. That's the single largest drop-off in the funnel. We'd like to spend 15 minutes with your team to understand what's happening on those calls — is it pricing, underwriting fit, timing, or something else? The answer tells us exactly which filter to tighten next.
→ Expected impact: Targeted adjustments that resolve the #1 bottleneck in your funnel.
